## Thread 3: Desk Booking & Office Navigation

**Rachel Kim** · January 15, 2026 at 8:22 AM  
How does the desk booking system work? I'm coming into the Seattle office tomorrow and I'm a bit confused by DeskFlex.

> **Jordan Rivera** · January 15, 2026 at 8:35 AM  
> Go to **deskflex.zavainsurance.com** and log in with your Entra ID. Pick your floor and date, then click on an available desk (they're color-coded — green = available, red = booked). Book at least 24 hours in advance since the good spots go fast on Tuesdays.

> **Sophie Laurent** · January 15, 2026 at 8:48 AM  
> Pro tip: the desks near the windows on the 5th floor have the best view of the waterfront, but they're drafty in winter. I prefer the 4th floor — closer to the cafeteria and the collaboration spaces are right there.

> **Marcus Chen** · January 15, 2026 at 9:05 AM  
> Also, each floor has a **"neighborhood"** setup — try to book on the same floor as your team. Ask your manager which floor your team usually sits on. Engineering is mostly 6th floor, Product is 4th, and HR is on 3.

> **Rachel Kim** · January 15, 2026 at 9:12 AM  
> Super helpful! What about parking? I'm driving in.

> **Derek Washington** · January 15, 2026 at 9:30 AM  
> Parking garage entrance is on the west side of the building off Waterfront Ave. Your badge works on the garage gate. First come first served — if you're arriving after 9:30 AM the garage fills up fast on Tuesdays and Wednesdays. There's also a public lot two blocks south for $15/day as a backup.

> **Lin Zhang** · January 15, 2026 at 9:45 AM  
> Don't forget to clean up your desk before you leave! The clean-desk policy is real — Facilities will put your stuff in a bin if you leave things overnight. I learned this the hard way with my favorite mug 😅

## Thread 4: VPN and Remote Work Setup

**Tomás García** · January 20, 2026 at 3:17 PM  
Is anyone else having trouble with the VPN dropping every 30 minutes or so? I'm working from home today and it's killing my productivity.

> **Aisha Thompson** · January 20, 2026 at 3:28 PM  
> Had the same issue last week! Try this: Open AnyConnect, go to **Settings → Preferences** and uncheck "Block connections to untrusted servers." That fixed it for me.

> **Marcus Chen** · January 20, 2026 at 3:35 PM  
> Also make sure Zscaler isn't conflicting with it. I had to temporarily **pause Zscaler**, connect the VPN, then re-enable Zscaler. IT told me there's a fix coming in the next AnyConnect update.

> **Sophie Laurent** · January 20, 2026 at 3:42 PM  
> Quick reminder that you actually **don't need VPN** for most things. Teams, Outlook, SharePoint, OneDrive, Workday, Concur — they all work without VPN. I only connect when I need Jira, Confluence, or internal dev environments.

> **Tomás García** · January 20, 2026 at 3:55 PM  
> @Sophie oh wow, that's a game changer. I've been running VPN all day for no reason. Thanks!

> **Jordan Rivera** · January 20, 2026 at 4:10 PM  
> Yeah the split tunnel setup means only internal traffic goes through VPN. Your regular internet stays on your home network. Battery life and speed are way better when you only connect when you actually need internal stuff.

> **Priya Mehta** · January 20, 2026 at 4:25 PM  
> Related tip — if you're on the **Essential Plan (HDHP)** and trying to access the Fidelity HSA portal, you need to go through **netbenefits.fidelity.com** directly, NOT through the Workday link. The Workday link sometimes loops on VPN.

## Thread 5: Benefits Enrollment Questions

**Rachel Kim** · January 22, 2026 at 10:05 AM  
I'm trying to decide between the three health plans. Has anyone done the math on which one makes sense for a single person in their late 20s who's generally healthy?

> **Marcus Chen** · January 22, 2026 at 10:20 AM  
> I went with the **Essential Plan (HDHP)** and the HSA. The premium is only $85/month, and Zava contributes $750/year to your HSA on top of what you put in. If you're healthy and don't go to the doctor much, you'll come out way ahead. Plus the HSA money rolls over forever — it's basically a stealth retirement account.

> **Aisha Thompson** · January 22, 2026 at 10:35 AM  
> I used the **Code Interpreter in the HR Buddy agent** to run the numbers! If you expect ~4 doctor visits and 1 specialist visit per year, the Essential Plan saves you about $1,100/year compared to the Enhanced Plan after accounting for the HSA contribution. But if you have a surgery or major procedure planned, the Premium Plan wins because of the low deductible ($500).

> **Sophie Laurent** · January 22, 2026 at 10:48 AM  
> Don't forget about the **$1,200 wellness stipend**! It's separate from health insurance. I'm using mine for a gym membership ($50/month) and Headspace is already free through the company. Between the stipend and the free Teladoc visits, the Essential Plan is plenty for me.

> **Derek Washington** · January 22, 2026 at 11:02 AM  
> For anyone with a family — I went Premium Plan. With kids, the copays add up fast and the $500 deductible vs $3,000 on Essential makes a huge difference. The family premium is steep ($895/mo) but the out-of-pocket max is only $5,000 which gives me peace of mind.

> **Lin Zhang** · January 22, 2026 at 11:15 AM  
> Heads up — enrollment deadline is **30 days from your start date**, not 30 business days. I almost missed mine. Also, you need your dependents' Social Security numbers and dates of birth ready when you enroll in Workday. Don't be like me scrambling to find them at the last minute 😂

> **Rachel Kim** · January 22, 2026 at 11:30 AM

## Thread 7: IT Setup — Mac Users

**Lin Zhang** · February 3, 2026 at 11:30 AM  
Any other Mac users here? The Intune enrollment seems stuck at "Installing management profile" for the last 20 minutes. Is that normal?

> **Aisha Thompson** · February 3, 2026 at 11:42 AM  
> Yes, that's normal! Mine took about **30 minutes** on the management profile step. Don't close the lid or restart — just let it do its thing. You'll get a notification when it's done.

> **Jordan Rivera** · February 3, 2026 at 11:55 AM  
> After Intune finishes, open **Self Service** (the Jamf app — it'll be in your Applications folder) to install anything else you need. The first time you open it, it takes a minute to sync all the available apps.

> **Marcus Chen** · February 3, 2026 at 12:08 PM  
> Mac tip: the **CalDigit TS4** dock that comes with the MacBook can be finicky. If your external monitor isn't being detected, try unplugging the Thunderbolt cable, waiting 10 seconds, then plugging it back in. Also make sure you're using the cable that came with the dock, not a random USB-C cable.

> **Lin Zhang** · February 3, 2026 at 12:15 PM  
> It just finished! Thanks for the patience tip. Now installing apps from Self Service. 

> **Sophie Laurent** · February 3, 2026 at 12:30 PM  
> One more Mac thing — if you're an engineer, don't install Homebrew until after the Jamf enrollment completes. I had a conflict with the managed Python installation that took IT 2 hours to sort out. Install Homebrew after everything else is set up and it works fine.

> **Priya Mehta** · February 3, 2026 at 1:05 PM  
> Also for Mac users — the **Microsoft Teams desktop app** is way better than the browser version. Screen sharing, background blur, and the meeting reactions all work more reliably in the app. The Self Service version is pre-configured with our SSO so you don't have to fiddle with settings.

## Thread 9: Remote Work Tips & Home Office Setup
